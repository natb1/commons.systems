# Underappreciated Advantages of Agentic Coding

Common [value propositions for agentic coding](https://claude.com/blog/key-benefits-transitioning-agentic-coding) overlook an important strategic advantage.

This blog is built to my own specification - it has few dependencies on third-party platforms, and it uses the technologies I like and am comfortable maintaining. The dependencies it does have are deliberate. This blog relies on standards that are open source and freely available (HTML, JavaScript, golang, git), or measured choices to adopt third-party infrastructure with deliberate lock in costs (GitHub, Firebase, Claude).

> The dependencies of this blog are measured and deliberate.

Coding agents are reaching a level of efficacy where they are changing how technology like this blog is deployed. There will be a new breed of platforms seeking to monetize this trend by *enabling organizations to leverage* the new technology, and by doing so position their platform as the new gatekeepers. Platforms will *accelerate adoption* with a new generation of low code, automation, and governance solutions. To the extent that the promise of this new technology is to recover agency in technology deployment, these platforms will fail to deliver on that promise.

> A key advantage of coding agents is to recover agency in technology deployment.

Agency is not recovered with naive delegation to coding agents. That approach neglects the knowledge work that integrates professional experience to build software with more foresight. Likewise, if the agents are only augmenting business as usual, it won't result in the velocities needed to decouple solutions from legacy dependencies. I will not be able to build a blog site without my business becoming a blog site business.

When delegating implementation work to coding agents the recovered time must be used for augmented planning and design workflows. The time to execute may be similar, but the result is a multiplication of a developer's professional experience resulting in maintainability of solutions on par with third-party products.

> The promise of coding agents is realized through combined: delegation of labor and augmentation of knowledge workers.
